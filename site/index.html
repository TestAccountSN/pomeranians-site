Meet the Pomeranian

The Pomeranian — affectionately called the "Pom" — is a tiny toy breed descended from large sled-pulling Spitz dogs of the Arctic. Named after the Pomerania region (today part of Poland and Germany), these dogs were bred down to size and became beloved companions. Queen Victoria famously adored them, sparking a craze that shrank the breed to the pint-sized fluffball we know today.

Personality & Traits

🦊 Foxy & Fluffy

A signature double coat, plumed tail, and alert fox-like face make Poms instantly recognizable.

🧠 Smart & Bold

Highly intelligent, curious, and confident — they often act much bigger than they are.

❤️ Loyal Companions

Affectionate and lively, they bond closely with their people and make great lap dogs.

🔔 Little Watchdogs

Alert and vocal, Poms are quick to announce visitors despite their small stature.

Caring for Your Pom

Grooming

Brush the thick double coat 2–3× a week to prevent mats and manage shedding.

Exercise

Short daily walks and play sessions keep them happy — they're energetic but small.

Diet

Small, measured portions of quality food; Poms can gain weight easily.

Health

Watch dental care and joints (luxating patella); regular vet checkups matter.

Fun Facts

  • 🚢 Two Pomeranians reportedly survived the Titanic in 1912.
  • 👑 Queen Victoria kept many Poms and helped popularize the smaller size.
  • 🎨 Pomeranians come in over 20 coat colors — orange is the most iconic.
  • 🐺 They're true Spitz dogs, related to Huskies and Samoyeds.
  • 🎓 Smart enough to excel at agility and trick training.
